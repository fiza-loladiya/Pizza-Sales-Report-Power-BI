# 🍕 Pizza Sales Report Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

Running a food business without data is basically guessing with extra toppings.

This project was built to analyze pizza sales performance and turn raw transaction data into a clear, interactive business dashboard using **Power BI**. The goal was to understand how the business was performing across revenue, orders, product categories, pizza sizes, and top/bottom-selling items.

Instead of looking at scattered sales numbers, this dashboard gives one complete view of business health. It helps answer practical questions like:

- When do customers place the most orders?
- Which pizza categories drive the most sales?
- What sizes are most popular?
- Which pizzas are performing best and worst?
- How can the business improve menu strategy and sales decisions?


## 🌍 Situation

A pizza business generates a large number of transactions every day, but raw data alone does not help decision-making.

Sales teams and business managers need more than just total revenue. They need to know:

- Which products are bringing in the most money
- Which items are underperforming
- Which days and months are busiest
- What customer ordering patterns look like

Without a proper dashboard, it becomes difficult to identify sales trends, customer preferences, and product-level performance.

That was the business problem this project aimed to solve.


## 🎯 Task

My task was to build an interactive dashboard in **Power BI** that could:

- Track overall sales performance using important KPIs
- Show daily and monthly order trends
- Compare category-wise and size-wise sales contribution
- Identify best-selling and worst-selling pizzas
- Make the data simple enough for business users to understand quickly

The dashboard needed to be clean, interactive, and decision-focused.


## 🛠️ Action

I used **Power BI** to design a two-page interactive sales report.

### 1. Built the Main Dashboard

The home page was designed to give a quick summary of business performance through key metrics such as:

- **Total Revenue**
- **Average Order Value**
- **Total Pizzas Sold**
- **Total Orders**
- **Average Pizzas per Order**

This gave an instant overview of how the pizza business was performing.

### 2. Analyzed Sales Trends Over Time

To understand customer order behavior, I added:

- **Daily trend for total orders**
- **Monthly trend for total orders**

This made it easy to spot peak sales periods. For example, weekends performed better, especially **Friday and Saturday evenings**, while **July and January** showed high order activity.

### 3. Studied Product Mix and Customer Preference

To understand what customers preferred, I created visuals for:

- **% of sales by pizza category**
- **% of sales by pizza size**
- **Total pizzas sold by pizza category**

This helped reveal which categories and sizes contributed most to revenue and overall sales volume.

### 4. Created a Best/Worst Seller Page

A separate page was built to dig deeper into product-level performance.

This page highlights:

- **Top 5 pizzas by revenue**
- **Top 5 pizzas by quantity**
- **Top 5 pizzas by total orders**
- **Bottom 5 pizzas by revenue**
- **Bottom 5 pizzas by quantity**
- **Bottom 5 pizzas by total orders**

This view helps identify high-performing menu items and weak products that may need better promotion, pricing changes, or menu reconsideration.

### 5. Added Interactivity

To make the report more useful, I included:

- **Category filter**
- **Date range filter**
- **Navigation buttons between pages**

This allows users to explore the data dynamically instead of relying on a static report.


## 📊 Result

The final dashboard delivered a clear and interactive view of pizza sales performance.

### Key Metrics

- **Total Revenue:** 817.86K  
- **Average Order Value:** 38.31  
- **Total Pizzas Sold:** 49,574  
- **Total Orders:** 21,350  
- **Average Pizzas per Order:** 2.32  

### Key Insights

- **Weekend sales were the strongest**, especially on **Friday and Saturday**
- **July and January** recorded the highest order volume
- The **Classic category** contributed the most to total sales and orders
- **Large-size pizzas** generated the highest share of sales
- Some pizzas consistently performed well across revenue, quantity, and orders
- A few pizzas appeared repeatedly among bottom performers, suggesting low customer demand or weak product positioning

### Business Value

This dashboard can support business decisions in several ways:

- Helps management identify peak sales periods for better staffing
- Shows which pizza categories and sizes deserve more focus
- Makes it easier to promote top-performing products
- Helps review weak-selling items for pricing, recipe, or menu optimization
- Provides one interactive report for quick performance monitoring
